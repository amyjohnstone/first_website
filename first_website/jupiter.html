Jupiter


The king of the planets. It is the first of the Jovian planets. Jovian is just a classification name given to gas giants. Jupiter is twice as large as the all the other planets masses combined. During the formation of the planets, Jupiter gathered more than half of the planetary material in the solar system that was left over by the Sun’s formation. The contents of Jupiter are similar to that of a star – hydrogen and helium - but Jupiter is not massive enough to start nuclear processes in its core. With such a large mass Jupiter has accumulated many natural satellites (i.e. moons), 79 to be exact.


Like Saturn, Jupiter also has rings but these are very faint and less interesting as they are made of dust not ice.
