Mars


The only other planet we have explored in detail. The red planet gets its name from the layer of reddish dust on the surface.

Why is Mars red?

Mars is not only red; it is in fact brown, gold and tan. The reddish tinge comes from oxidisation (rusting) of iron on the planet. The oxidised dust particles are distributed through the atmosphere and thus scattered across the planet, making it overall look red from a distance.


Water?

Information gathered by rovers on the surface of Mars has suggested that there was once water on the planet. This leads to the idea that there might have been life on Mars a long time ago but not anymore.
