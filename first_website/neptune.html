Neptune


The final planet in the solar system is so far away that it is not visible to the naked eye. Hence, it was not discovered by telescope but with mathematics. Urbain Le Verrier used mathematics to infer that the deviation of Uranus’ orbit was caused by the presence of a large object – the planet Neptune. Johann Galle first observed the planet in 1846.


Due to its great distance from the Sun, it receives little sunlight. The brightest day on Neptune would seem like twilight to us.
