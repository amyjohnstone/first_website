Space Travel


The first person in space was cosmonaut Yuri Gagarin, who completed one orbit of the Earth in 1961. Following that, the USA landed 3 men on the Moon in 1969. We haven’t gone any further than our satellite but as you know, everything in space is far away. The next goal is to have humans on Mars. When exactly this will be achieved is unclear, but plans are being drafted.


How do you build a spacecraft?

A rocket designed for human space travel must meet the following criteria:

  • Astronauts must have some control over the spacecraft
  • It must be tolerant of failure
  • The spacecraft must protect the crew even if unrecoverable failure occurs
  • The probability of failure upon ascent is 1 in 500
  • The probability of failure upon descent is 1 in 500


Taking into all spacecraft built and sent to space - both manned and unmanned - humans have done a lot over the past few decades. . Technology is constantly improving, and this means unmanned spacecraft can travel further, last longer and gather and transmit more information.


Sad to say that a lot of telescopes and probes we send out never come home. . Since they travel such large distances it is easier just to switch them off and let them drift through the cosmos than to spend money on fuel to bring them back. . For example, NASA’s probe Voyager 1 which was launched in 1977 is now 13 billion miles away from Earth – outside the solar system – and it still transmits information back to Earth!
