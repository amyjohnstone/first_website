The Sun

The star that brought life to our planet is called the Sun. It is a main sequence star. A main sequence star forms helium from hydrogen in its core via nuclear fusion.


Our Sun is estimated to be around 4.6 billion years old – it sounds ancient, but it is in fact only halfway through its life cycle!


The Sun is at the heart of our Solar System. This is not by chance – the Sun is the most massive body in our neighbourhood and therefore has the strongest gravitational attraction, causing all smaller celestial bodies to orbit it.
