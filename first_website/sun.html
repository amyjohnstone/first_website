The Sun

Our Sun is unusual in the fact that it is not in a binary pair with a twin star like most main sequence stars of this size

The Sun


The star that brought life to our planet is called the Sun. It is a main sequence star. A main sequence star forms helium from hydrogen in its core via nuclear fusion.


The Sun is at the heart of our Solar System. This is not by chance – the Sun is the most massive body in our neighbourhood (it contains 98% of the total mass in the entire solar system) and therefore has the strongest gravitational attraction, causing all smaller celestial bodies to orbit it.


Our Sun is estimated to be around 4.6 billion years old – it sounds ancient, but it is in fact only halfway through its life cycle!

It is believed that the Sun and the rest of the solar system formed from a large cloud of gas and dust called the solar nebula. . As the nebula got bigger it collapsed due to its own gravity, while spinning faster and flattening into a disk. Most of this material formed the Sun.


In a few billion years the Sun will expend all its nuclear fuel and become a red giant. Following that, the outer layers of the star will fall off and the remains will collapse to create a white dwarf. Over time this hot dense object will cool down and dim to form a black dwarf.
